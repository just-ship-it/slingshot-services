# B2 — Compressed-overnight fade-the-first-break (NQ, 1s-honest)

**Verdict: DEAD — refuted in dev.** All 48 grid configs on 2021–2024 lost money
(PF 0.14–0.81, best config 0/4 years positive). Hold sweep and the first-hour
overlay did not rescue it. The census effect (compressed ON → 38% both-extremes-
broken days) is real but does NOT convert into a fade edge at the first break.

## Design

- Universe: days whose ON range (18:00→09:29 ET, single symbol, matching RTH
  symbol) ≤ trailing 33.3rd percentile of the PRIOR 250 days' ON ranges
  (min 100 days history; strictly excludes today — knowable at 09:30). Full RTH,
  no roll days, atr14_prior known. Dev universe: 284 days.
- Signal: FIRST 1s breach after 09:30 of ON high+B / ON low−B, B∈{3,8,15},
  observed at breach-bar close (+1s). One trade/day, fade the break.
- Entries: `lim` limit AT the ON extreme placed at breach+1s (conservative: fills
  at the limit exactly even when marketable — i.e., worst-case price), cancel
  60m after breach; `cls1m` market entry (next 1s open ±0.25 adverse) after the
  first ET 1m close back inside the ON range, cancel 60m, no entries after 15:15.
- Stop: post-breach adverse extreme (breach bar→entry bar) + buffer; `b5`=5pt,
  `bv`=0.05×ATR14. Targets (limit, exact): `opp` opposite ON extreme, `mid` ON
  midpoint, `r1`/`r2` = 1×/2× entry-to-stop risk. Max hold H=120m in the main
  grid (H∈{60,180} swept on the best family), hard flat 15:45 ET.
- Sim contract identical to B1 (see B1-gap-fill.md): 1s walks from fill instant,
  stop slips 0.5, market/time exits 0.25, same-1s-bar stop+target = STOP, $5 RT,
  $20/pt. Same RTH 1s cache and self-computed day features.
- Sharpe convention: daily PnL over all full-RTH days in period, ×√252.

## Failure mode (honest read)

- First breaks out of compressed overnight ranges CONTINUE far more than they
  reverse cleanly. `lim`-at-the-extreme entries are the disaster case (PF
  0.14–0.67, WR 5–41%): on breakout-trend days you are filled instantly at the
  worst price and run over; the census's "both extremes break 38% of the time"
  offers no timing — the opposite extreme often trades hours later or the next
  leg, far beyond any survivable stop.
- Waiting for a 1m close back inside (`cls1m`) improves PF to ~0.65–0.81 but
  stop% is still 43–67%: the post-breach extreme + buffer stop is repeatedly
  taken out before mid/opposite targets, and R-multiple targets don't fix the
  geometry either.
- Uniformity of failure (every year 2021–2024 negative for the best family, all
  three B values, both entries, both stops, all four targets) makes this a
  refutation of the trade construction, not a parameter-tuning miss.
- 2023 dominates the compressed-day count (130 of 284 dev days) because trailing
  terciles lag the 2022→2023 vol regime shift; even restricting attention to
  non-2023 years, no config family was positive across the remaining years.
