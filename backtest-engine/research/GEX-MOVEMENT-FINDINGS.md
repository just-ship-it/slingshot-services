# GEX Movement & Touch Reaction Study — Findings

**Window:** 2025-01-13 → 2026-01-23 (12 months, 269 trading days, NQ)
**GEX source:** `data/gex/nq-cbbo` (1m cbbo, post-bug-fix, trustworthy)
**Price space:** raw 1m contract w/ `filterPrimaryContract()`
**Scripts:** `gex-touch-reactions.js` (Track 1), `gex-level-migration.js` (Track 2)

---

## TL;DR — REVISED 2026-05-06 after lookahead correction

- **Critical bug found mid-study:** the GEX-snapshot bucketing in `scripts/generate-intraday-gex.py` floors timestamps to 15-min boundaries and keeps the **last** OHLCV close in each bucket. A snapshot labeled `T:00` actually reflects spot at `~T:14:59`. Most snapshot fields (nq_spot, multiplier, all walls/flip in NQ space, regime, gamma_imbalance) are 14 min forward-looking relative to their label.
- **Track 2 (migration):** the headline `d_gamma_imbalance` r=0.67 was lookahead. **With entry deferred 16 min after the snapshot timestamp, r drops to −0.03 and Q5 mean drops from +43pt to −1.3pt. No surviving signal.**
- **Track 1 (touches):** Same bucketing bias. With snapshot lag enforced (only use snapshots whose data window ends before the candle), the "rally into call wall" finding collapses from 86.9% / +34pt to **56.7% / +11pt at n=97**. Most other level reactions degrade similarly. A handful of regime-conditional setups (S4/S5 in negative regime) retain a modest edge but nothing exceptional.
- **Net recommendation:** **Do NOT build the `gex-imbalance-shift` strategy as planned.** The signal does not exist under honest timing. Skip Tracks 3–4. We should save the lookahead finding to memory because it likely affects other GEX-driven backtests in this codebase.

The original (lookahead-biased) writeup is preserved below for reference; it is **not** a valid baseline.

## Lookahead correction details

`scripts/generate-intraday-gex.py:262-269` (in `load_ohlcv_for_date`):

```python
ts = pd.to_datetime(ts_str)
minute = (ts.minute // 15) * 15
bucket = ts.replace(minute=minute, second=0, microsecond=0)
bucket_key = bucket.isoformat()
prices[bucket_key] = close   # KEEPS LAST close in [T, T+15)
```

Verified empirically on 2025-06-02:
- Snapshot `T=08:00:00` has `nq_spot=21249.75`. NQ close at `08:14:00` = 21249.75 (bucket end).
- Snapshot `T=08:15:00` has `nq_spot=21255.50`. NQ close at `08:29:00` = 21255.50.
- Snapshot `T=08:30:00` has `nq_spot=21237.50`. NQ close at `08:44:00` = 21237.50.

So the fix in our research scripts is to require:
- **Track 1:** snapshot timestamp must satisfy `snap_ts <= candle_ts - 16min` (CLI flag: `--snap-lag-min 16`)
- **Track 2:** entry candle must be at `snapshot_ts + 16min` or later (CLI flag: `--entry-offset-min 16`)

16 minutes = 15 (bucket window) + 1 (safety).

This may affect other backtests in the codebase — notably anything reading from `data/gex/nq-cbbo/` or other intraday GEX directories generated by the same script. Our `iv-skew-gex` gold-standard backtest uses these snapshots; **need to verify whether the strategy's snapshot-lookup logic in `backtest-engine/src` already accounts for this lag**, otherwise the v8 "balanced" gold-standard numbers may also be inflated.

### Lookahead control

`d_gamma_imbalance` is partly a function of where spot is (gamma redistributes above/below as price moves). Concern: maybe it's just an inverted "did price drop in the last 15 min?" signal, and the forward return is mean reversion of that drop.

Tested by residualizing d_gamma_imbalance against backward_return:

| Horizon | n | r(imb, back) | r(back, fwd) | r(imb, fwd) | **r(resid, fwd)** |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 15m | 6,696 | 0.225 | 0.335 | 0.669 | **0.609** |
| 45m | 6,692 | 0.225 | 0.205 | 0.409 | **0.372** |
| 90m | 6,527 | 0.227 | 0.148 | 0.291 | **0.265** |

**~91% of the 15-min predictive power survives residualization (0.609 / 0.669).** d_gamma_imbalance carries information genuinely independent of recent price action.

### Other Track 2 signals worth noting

- **`d_flip` quintiles**: monotonic gradient, Q1 (largest down-shift) +10.4 / 62.7% WR, Q5 (largest up-shift) −10.7 / 37.6% WR. Smaller magnitude than imbalance but cleaner directional logic. **Flip moving DOWN = bullish next 15m** (and vice versa).
- **`flip_convergence`**: when flip migrates *toward* current price (Q5), forward 15m mean +16.2pt / 75.1% WR. When flip is migrating *away*, the move continues against you.
- **Regime extreme**: the 71 transitions in `strong_negative` regime had mean −22.2pt at 15min, −28.2 at 45m, −39.8 at 90m. Pure trend continuation when regime label is extreme negative.

## What the data is probably telling us

1. **Gamma-imbalance shift is the strongest single signal in the dataset.** When dealers' net gamma redistributes upward (d_gamma_imbalance > 0) — meaning more positive gamma above spot relative to below — price tends to rally over the next 15 minutes. The reverse holds for downward redistribution. This is consistent with dealer hedging mechanics: a shift in where dealers need to hedge changes the directional pressure they exert.

2. **The "rally-into-call-wall" setup is clean and tradable.** 87% bounce at n=61 over 12 months is rare in any literature.

3. **Most simple level-migration features (Δcall_wall, Δput_wall, Δwall_range, flip_dist) carry near-zero predictive power individually.** Only the *imbalance* of gamma above-vs-below price matters in this dataset.

---

## Caveats

- 12-month window only (post-2026-01-29 OHLCV is corrupted by the bucket-fragmentation bug).
- No transaction costs or slippage applied to the conditional means above. Real trade economics will degrade, especially the extreme-quintile means.
- Backward-return autocorrelation: the imbalance signal is only ~22% explained by backward return, but they're not zero. Strategy backtests must respect causality at the snapshot boundary.
- "Edge" in Track 1 is mfe − mae, an upper-bound proxy. A real strategy with fixed TP/SL will realize less.
- All of Track 2's `d_gamma_imbalance` analysis fires off 15-min snapshot boundaries. Live trading needs the 1-minute imbalance estimate from the data-service's exposure-calculator output.

## Recommendation

**Skip Tracks 3 and 4 for now and prototype two strategies:**

1. **`gex-imbalance-shift` (Track 2)**: At each 15-min snapshot boundary, compute Δ(gamma_imbalance). If the magnitude crosses a threshold derived from quintile cutoffs (e.g., top/bottom 20% of historical Δ-distribution), enter NQ in the direction of the shift. Target ~30–40pt, stop ~25–30pt, hold up to 15–45 min.

2. **`call-wall-fade` (Track 1)**: When NQ rallies from below into the day's call wall (within 5pt) AFTER a sustained move, enter short. Target the gamma flip or first support, stop above the wall by ~20pt.

Both are simple enough that engine integration is cheap. We'll learn whether the conditional-mean edges survive realistic execution. If either holds up, then revisit Tracks 3–4 to see if magnetism/cross-features amplify them.
