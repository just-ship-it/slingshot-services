                                             SUMMARY



           Overall, we’ve been monitoring a suspected bull-trap for about a month
           now, and with the price falling below the price point where we first thought
           the trap was starting, it seems fitting to say the trap has sprung.

           In its jaws, $SPY and $QQQ moved about 75% more-than-expected to the
           downside.

           The result is that VoEx-dailies for $SPY and $QQQ have both returned to
           the Propagation Zon. VoEx has stalled in its movement towards stability
           and looks poised to re-enter the P.Z. with VoEx-daily.

           The hedging environments see a vanna-unstable orientation for $SPY, and
           a sparsely-supportive environment for $QQQ.

           Put-selling is still near-zero, and is uninspiring.

           Market-wide gamma squeeze data show some up-tick in involvement –
           with growth in gamma squeeze counts noted, growth in the growth-rate of
           gamma squeezes, and a once-again-negative GTM.

           Overall, high risk for significant downside interspaced with choppiness.

Hello every-body!


Last week, we held out that the performance we had been witnessing for the past few weeks was
largely due to a bull-trap, and thus, there was expectation for eventual downside following the
jubilations. This eventual downside expectation slowly grew to imminent downside expectation
as the data worsened, and then, finally, the imminent downside grew to downside.
This process tested both our patience and $SPY, who faltered (unlike us [obviously because we
aren’t a stock index]) and lost ~2% this week while $QQQ, in a bid to not be out-done, contracted
~3%.
Let’s take a quick look at these percentages under the “normalization lens” to see just how much
these moves compared to the market’s expectations. For this, let’s use the T+4 EPR from Friday.
With that, we get something around 1.75x for $SPY and, coincidentally, 1.75x for $QQQ! In other
words, things moved about 75% more than the market was expecting. That’s a lot of movement!

This movement can be visualized below in the Hedging Heatmaps from last Friday, where we see
that, for the first time in a long time, both $SPY and $QQQ fell off the map!




It seems that once conditions settled inside of gamma neutrality, $SPY’s purchasing support was
poised to stall things for a bit, and perhaps it did – but ultimately, things gave way towards [$-,
IV+] for the remainder of the week.
So, now that our bull-trap has trapped, the main priority will be in weaving through the data with a
mind for if things are better off now that the market has blown off some steam, or, if the market
is set to continue the downside expected from before the bull trap.
So, let’s get started!
