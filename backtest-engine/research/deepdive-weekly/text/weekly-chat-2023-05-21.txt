The take-home message here is that now that both VoEx for $SPY and $QQQ are in the
Inhibition Zone, we should consider it bullish until VoEx Divergence or a VoEx Spike
occurs. Now, let's take a peek at VoEx Spikes.

VoEx Spikes are, well, spike complexes – these complexes can be either positive
(meaning the point is upwards facing) or negative (meaning the point is downwards
facing). VoEx Spikes in and of themselves can be more dynamic than VoEx Divergence.
The price outcome of a VoEx Spike is dependent on the amount (how much VoEx
changed), the direction of the VoEx Spike, and also its origin-value.

This is exemplified in the Market Scan PDF that showcases the change in price
experienced by an equity over the next 5 days after a VoEx Spike, depending on if VoEx
was originally positive (VoEx started near or in the Inhibition Zone) or negative (VoEx
started near or in the Propagation Zone). The initially-positive VoExes are represented by
the gray line, and the initially-negative VoExes are represented by the blue line:

how large the VoEx Spike was and combining those values into the “Prediction”, which is
