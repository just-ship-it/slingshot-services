The benefit of having an identifiable price-insensitive party is that they act like a smoke-trail to
discern the wind direction, in this case, to discern the movement of the liquidity provider 1! You’ll
remember that hedging is a price-insensitive task (allegedly) and so, the transactions will naturally
occur where the source of liquidity is. Thus, we can “track” the movement or rather the general
direction of the winds moving towards the great-liquidity-attractor. Here, we see that the winds
are sweeping our heavy smoke trail (lots of selling) rapidly upwards and to the north-westernly
direction.
The benefit of this rather unique situation2 is two-fold: the first is it tells us (without even LDPM/S!)
that the source of liquidity is likely at or near the organic ask. This tells us that the transactions
occurring are predominately occurring via an eager bid! An eager-bid driven price appreciation
puts incredible risk for a liquidity vacuum formation, which we should look for when we
investigate LDPM/S on ES (spoiler: cant really find one!). Secondly, such a situation tells us that
an eager ask is currently indistinguishable from an organic ask.
Spooky! Because of the presumed location of the liquidity-source (namely it being at or within the
organic ask), this tells us that effectively the price-sensitive eager asks (those looking to take
profits and or those looking to short) would also be swept upwards.
