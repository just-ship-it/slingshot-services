In other words, VoEx Divergence is forming on $SPY and $QQQ – one of the heralding trumpets
to the end of BUPO.
Typically, VoEx Divergence should be monitored for when VoEx-trend leaves the Inhibition Zone
as a definitive trigger towards downside3. So, for now, the divergence will be monitored with the
expectation of forming downside in the near future.
This near-future, however, isn’t now and as such, BUPO is still dominant – even if weakened by
the presence of divergence. Since VoEx is still within the Inhibition Zone for both $SPY and
$QQQ, BUPO (of the Bullish variety) is still the most prudent outlook.
How does this outlook fare in the current hedging environments?




With conditions being much-the-same (local selling pressure, vanna-unstable orientation,
gamma neutrality extending $+/IV-) we should expect much-the-same influence from the
hedging environment: not terribly much.
So far, the market has been absorbing the additional (light) selling pressure and with gamma
neutrality extending in a $+/IV- path, it doesn’t seem that the hedging environment is well-poised
to prevent bullish activity. However, as can be expected from a vanna-unstable orientation, the
hedging environments are not positioned well to provide any purchasing support in the event of
a $-/IV+ move, either.
In short: conditions aren’t restrictive for any price appreciation but this is concurrent to a lack of
support for any price depreciation.
So, with VoEx in tentatively-bullish mode and hedging environments just existing, how do these
conditions affect the temperaments of our put-selling friends? Are they excited for the start of
the new-year or do they find themselves remembering on markets past?
