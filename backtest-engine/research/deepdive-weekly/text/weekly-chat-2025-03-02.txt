                                           SUMMARY



          Last week saw substantial movements, with Friday coming to enact a
          mean-reversion regime. The net-effect of this was to move VoEx into the
          Propagation Zone for both $SPY and $QQQ and increase gamma
          squeezes throughout the market.

          An additional consequence of this turbulence is the production of a vanna
          orientation for $SPY’s hedging environment and a gamma-squeeze
          orientation for $QQQ’s environment.

          Friday’s end-of-week moves prompted modest put-selling, however due to
          the relative minimalist response, it is difficult to ascribe strong conviction
          to these data.

          MWGS data shows a 17% growth in gamma squeezes month-over-month,
          coinciding with a rebound in the growth-curve. Additionally, these
          increases were met with a deepening, negative GTM. In conjunction, this
          raises the expectation for continued GS growth.

          An LDPM/S assessment suggests that a mean-reversion week should
          terminate at about 3% up, while the current downside target remains at
          LDOM-987, which is located at approximately -10% for both ES and NQ.
