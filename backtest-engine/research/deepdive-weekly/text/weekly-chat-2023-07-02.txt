                                         SUMMARY


           This week, VoEx for $SPY and $QQQ have risen yet-again into the
           Inhibition Zone, in a rare sense of cohesion, they both suggest BUPO.

           The hedging environments are providing immediate and local selling
           pressure and provides little-to-no purchasing support in a spot-down, IV-
           up – a particularly concerning arrangement considering the market’s
           mostly larger-than-expected movements throughout the week putting an
           increased risk of mean reversion on the table.

           Per MWGSC, however, the market is in the process of shedding excess
           gamma squeezes – a sign of returning stability, and GTM is printing
           negative values once again.

           Our new and developing tool, the GTM-Velocity is showing sustained but
           weakening bid as the data are starting to show an increasingly aggressive
           ask.

           In combination, this solidifies the idea that the rogue bullish wave is
           resting on unstable grounds, and the medium-to-longer term outlook of a
           market downturn is still reasonable.

Last week, we left off with the idea that we were approaching ~ the storm ~ but the data
were suggestive that a rogue wave (i.e.: bullish run) was manifesting. This ideology was
mainly supported by VoEx, and the hedging environments.

This was an apt description for the week ahead, with $SPY moving +2.32%, and $QQQ
moving +1.90%.

With the shortened week before us, we simply need to check how our rogue bullish wave
is performing and when we can expect the resumption of the more longer-term outlook
(bearishness).

Let’s dive in!

In terms of VoEx, the week started with VoEx diving into the Propagation Zone on both
$SPY and $QQQ, only to rapidly rebound the next day (Tuesday, 06/27/2023):




Admittedly, not the most routine of VoEx behavior, but it isn’t un-precedented. This

This rapid movement is the genesis of the mantra: an unstable VoEx is an unstable stock;
simply due to the fact that this type of behavior does not occur at or around stable stocks
(those that are simply appreciating-along), but rather, occur when things are getting
spicey.

After Monday’s dip into the Propagation Zone, this was the signal-long-awaited such that
downside would be here, but truthfully, with the purchasing support located on the
hedging environments, I elected to wait a day to see how the market would respond.

Should the market chew through the purchasing support for one more day, while VoEx
dove deeper into the P.Z., I was prepared to commit to full-downside; however, should the
market appreciate, and VoEx move back out of the P.Z., I was prepared to continue the
bullish-rogue-wave that was anticipated.

So, with Tuesday’s recovery, and VoExes’ resumption into the I.Z., it seemed the market
was planning for the latter.
