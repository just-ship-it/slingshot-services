Last week, we left off with the notion that there might be a “flat-to-greenish” bounce in the week,
as evidenced by some VoEx Divergence and a supportive hedging environment.
That outlook proved to be … technically correct, but truthfully, it doesn’t feel like it! The week took
the inch of a bull-bounce and gone to the moon with it, as evidenced by $SPY moving 2.65% for
the week, along with $QQQ’s 3.94%
With such big moves, it can be worthwhile to normalize them into a number that helps us
understand the relative magnitude of these moves. So, like usual, let’s check out the price-change
normalized against the EPR for the week to see just how big these moves were.
Doing this, we get 1.36 for $SPY, and 1.60 for $QQQ – so $SPY moved 136% more than the market
was expecting from last Friday, while $QQQ moved 160% more! Not too shabby.
Looking at these moves through the gamma hedging environments shows a pretty interesting
story.




The start-of-the-week for $QQQ saw the initiation of a robust push into selling pressure, after
which it seems the index mostly floated back towards gamma neutrality, only to end the week
with one final push.
For $SPY, on the other hand, it seems a similar route was attempted , but didn’t quite manage to
get past the selling pressure as well as $QQQ – perhaps contributing to the 20% difference in
overall weekly price movement between the two indexes.
Thus, it would seem this week provided a bounce and, on the surface, a reasonable-enough show-
of-force to warrant a pause and re-investigation as to how long this dip is expected to dipeth.
So, let’s get started!
