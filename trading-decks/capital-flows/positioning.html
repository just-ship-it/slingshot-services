THE THING ON EVERYONE'S CALENDAR

WEDNESDAY. THE WHOLE TAPE IS STARING AT IT.

One catalyst, fixed on the calendar, with an untested chair setting the tone. Everybody knows it's coming. That's exactly why the game isn't the print — it's the lean going in.
WHAT ALMOST EVERYONE IS DOING

THE WHOLE FEED IS WRITING THE SAME IF → THEN.

IF Warsh sounds hawkish…
→ then sell, risk-off
IF the dots move up…
→ then it's over
IF he hints the next move is a cut…
→ then risk-on, rip
IF he sounds worried on inflation…
→ then risk-off
IF he says "data-dependent"…
→ then fade it
IF the presser is calm…
→ then melt-up
Every one of these is a plan to react — to wait for the print and then scramble for a take. Nobody in this pile is asking the question that actually pays.
THE PART THEY DON'T SEE

THE REACTIVE CROWD IS THE LIQUIDITY.

When the crowd finally "reacts," they're buying the top or selling the bottom to someone. That someone is whoever did the work beforehand. You want to be the fill — not the chaser.

KNOW WHO'S ON THE OTHER SIDE

WHO'S POSITIONED — AND WHO MOVES THE TAPE.

▸ INSTITUTIONS
Positioned early. Hedged. Thinking in scenarios and size. Often already in the trade you're just now noticing.
▸ RETAIL
Positioned late. Headline-driven. Herding into the obvious take after it's printed — the reactive flow.
Get a read on how each camp is leaning — and, just as important, who's actually setting price into the event. When a crowded, one-sided book is driving, the surprise that hurts it most is the one to plan around.
REDUNDANCY PLANNING

MAP THE RANGE. NOT ONE PREDICTION.

Don't marry one call. Lay out the branches, weight them, and tag each with the positioning that makes it move. Redundancy planning is holding the tree — not betting the leaf.
YOUR CURRENT BOOK DECIDES YOUR MOVE

ARE YOU ALREADY POSITIONED?

The event means something different depending on what you're already holding. Carrying a thesis? Defend it. Flat? It's an opportunity, not an obligation.
TWO CLEAN PATHS — PICK ON PURPOSE

HEDGE THE THESIS — OR USE THE FALLOUT.

◆ IF YOU'RE POSITIONED → HEDGE
Name exactly what you're protecting and over what horizon. Size the hedge to the scenario that breaks you, not every wiggle. Accept it's a cost of carrying conviction through a known landmine.
Define the breakSize to the tailKeep the core thesis
◆ IF YOU'RE FLAT → GET POSITIONED
Two ways in: pre-position if you genuinely have an edge on the range and the lean — or wait for the overreaction and let the reactive crowd hand you the price. Either way it's deliberate, not a flinch.
Pre · if you have the edgePost · buy the panicNever · chase the move
Both are active choices made before the bell. The one move that isn't on the menu is improvising live.
