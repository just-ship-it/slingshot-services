NVIDIA holds ~81-92% of AI accelerators, hit ~$5T market cap, and projects $1T of Blackwell + Rubin sales across '26-'27 . Protected by 15+ years of CUDA. The real threat is custom silicon from its own customers — TPU, Trainium, Maia — forecast to be ~50% of capacity by 2030. The most concentrated rent in tech right now — and the most crowded trade.

TSMC owns ~70% of all foundry and well into the 90%s on advanced AI nodes , running ~66% gross margins with capacity sold out before mass production. '26 capex of $52-56B is the most aggressive in foundry history. Samsung and Intel are measurably behind on yield. If NVIDIA is the toll booth, TSMC owns the only road that reaches it.

Water / Cooling COMMODITY
Every data center is a physical object — miles of copper, rare-earth magnets, specialty process gases, and enormous volumes of cooling water. Lowest margin, most cyclical — but the true upstream. Real-economy demand created entirely by the digital boom above. The second-order AI trade most people never get to.
