WHERE IT ALL STARTS

EVERYTHING DOWNSTREAM
SURVIVES ON ONE STREAM

A consumer sub. A seat license. A metered API call. That's it. Every chip foundry, every datacenter, every model lab — they all live or die on a slice of that one stream.
THE REAL QUESTION

IS THE FAUCET WIDENING
FAST ENOUGH FOR THE BILL?

The "is AI a bubble" debate isn't really about AI. It's about whether the faucet on the left keeps widening fast enough to pay the bill on the right. That's the only question that matters.
THE CAPITAL INTERMEDIARY

THE HYPERSCALERS AREN'T A LAYER.
THEY'RE THE VALVE.

Not pure plays. Closer to the consumer than to inference — legacy businesses pay the bills while AI capex leaves the balance sheet through SPVs, off-BS debt, and suspended buybacks. The bill itself washes over every layer downstream.

Applications & Agents
THE SURFACE · WHAT USERS ACTUALLY TOUCH
CursorPRIV PerplexityPRIV HarveyPRIV GleanPRIV Microsoft CopilotMSFT Adobe FireflyADBE
Closest to the user, furthest from a moat. Most are a thin layer of UX over someone else's model. Winners earn defensibility through proprietary data, workflow lock-in, or distribution. High revenue is easy here. Keeping it is the hard part.
MARGIN: CONTESTED · WRAPPER RISK
02
Foundation Models
THE LABS · WHERE THE FRONTIER IS TRAINED
OpenAI~$850B Anthropic~$965B Google DeepMindGOOGL Meta AIMETA xAIPRIV MistralPRIV
The headline names — and the most capital-hungry layer. Anthropic ~$47B run-rate; OpenAI $13B+ heading to $100B target. But most of it flows straight down to compute. Brand and scale are real moats; frontier capability is rented from the layers below.
MARGIN: THIN / NEGATIVE · CASH BURN
03
Cloud & Inference
THE TOLL ROAD · RENTED COMPUTE BY THE HOUR
Oracle OCIORCL CoreWeaveCRWV NebiusNBIS IRENIREN
The pure-play neoclouds — companies whose entire business is renting GPUs at scale. Oracle is the largest non-hyperscaler AI cloud; CoreWeave, Nebius, and IREN are dedicated GPU-hosting plays where AI is the whole story, not a side bet. The hyperscalers' clouds live one slide up — they're the valve, not pure inference plays.
MARGIN: STRONG · CAPEX-HEAVY
04
Interconnect & Memory
THE WIRES & HBM · A GPU IS USELESS ALONE
BroadcomAVGO AristaANET Astera LabsALAB SK HynixKRX:000660 MicronMU CoherentCOHR
Quietly one of the best risk/rewards in the stack. A GPU needs networking (Broadcom, Arista) and high-bandwidth memory — and HBM is its own supply-constrained oligopoly. Broadcom also designs the custom AI chips for Google. Picks-and-shovels behind the picks-and-shovels.
MARGIN: STRONG · OLIGOPOLY
05
AI Accelerators
THE TOLL BOOTH · CENTER OF GRAVITY
NVIDIANVDA AMDAMD Google TPUGOOGL AWS TrainiumAMZN Microsoft MaiaMSFT
NVIDIA holds ~81-92% of AI accelerators, hit ~$5T market cap, and projects $1T of Blackwell + Rubin sales across '26-'27. Protected by 15+ years of CUDA. The real threat is custom silicon from its own customers — TPU, Trainium, Maia — forecast to be ~50% of capacity by 2030. The most concentrated rent in tech right now — and the most crowded trade.
MARGIN: MONOPOLY-LIKE · THE TOLL BOOTH
06
Foundry · Manufacturing
THE ONLY ROAD · NVIDIA DESIGNS, TSMC BUILDS
TSMCTSM Samsung FoundryKRX:005930 Intel FoundryINTC
TSMC owns ~70% of all foundry and well into the 90%s on advanced AI nodes, running ~66% gross margins with capacity sold out before mass production. '26 capex of $52-56B is the most aggressive in foundry history. Samsung and Intel are measurably behind on yield. If NVIDIA is the toll booth, TSMC owns the only road that reaches it.
MARGIN: WIDE MOAT · NEAR-MONOPOLY
07
Chip Equipment
THE BEDROCK · YOU CAN'T ROUTE AROUND PHYSICS
ASMLASML Applied MaterialsAMAT Lam ResearchLRCX KLAKLAC Tokyo ElectronTSE:8035
ASML is the only company on earth that makes EUV lithography — TSMC physically cannot print advanced chips without it. Applied, Lam and KLA round out a tight oligopoly for deposition, etch and inspection. The single hardest layer to disrupt in the entire flow.
MARGIN: DEEPEST CHOKEPOINT · SOLE SUPPLIER
08
Power & Cooling
THE NEW BOTTLENECK · YOU HAVE TO PLUG IT IN
ConstellationCEG VistraVST GE VernovaGEV VertivVRT EatonETN SchneiderEURONEXT:SU
AI data centers may consume ~5% of global electricity by 2030, and TSMC now says energy efficiency — not compute — is the main design constraint. That turns power generation and thermal/electrical gear into AI infrastructure plays. The market spent two years pricing chips. It's now waking up to the fact you have to plug them in.
MARGIN: RISING FAST · BINDING CONSTRAINT
09
Raw Inputs
THE SECOND-ORDER TRADE · COPPER · EARTH · WATER
CopperFCX · SCCO Rare EarthsMP Specialty GasesAPD · LIN Water / CoolingCOMMODITY
Every data center is a physical object — miles of copper, rare-earth magnets, specialty process gases, and enormous volumes of cooling water. Lowest margin, most cyclical — but the true upstream. Real-economy demand created entirely by the digital boom above. The second-order AI trade most people never get to.
MARGIN: CYCLICAL · UPSTREAM
